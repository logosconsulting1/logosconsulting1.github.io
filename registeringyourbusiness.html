Business Structure
Types of Structure:
Limited Liability Company (LLC)
Key things:
  • Single owner
  • 1-10 Employees
  • Annual fee required
  • Starting fee required
  • Good credible status

What is an LLC?
Individual business owners that run their own business and hire less than ten people as subcontractors/employees. There is an annual fee that you have to pay to the state your from and there is a starting fee that your state will charge you to register as an LLC.

For tax purposes, you have to file your LLC either under your SSN or EIN number and report profits/losses under your name.

Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)
Key things:
  • +2 business partners
  • 1-10 Employees
  • Annual fee required
  • Starting fee required
  • Good credible status

What is an LLP?
Two or more business owners that work together as partners in a business. Each partner may or may not hire subcontractors/employees for tasks. There is an annual fee that you have to pay to the state your from and there is a starting fee that your state will charge you to register as an LLP.

For tax purposes, each partner has to file under their personal social security number and report profits/losses that each partner incurred while in business each year.

Corporation (Inc.)
Key things:
  • Business run by shareholders
  • 10-100 Employees
  • Annual fee required
  • Starting fee required
  • Most credible status

What is a Corporation?
A corporation is a technically a big business run shareholders. This type of filing usually has between 1-100 employees. Corporations are also taxed at a corporate level. Company stock may also be given as an incentive to it's employees. This filing makes your business look the most credible in a business setting.

For tax purposes, corporations are filed as a separate tax entity. You can even add the word Inc. at the end of your business name. For example: Apple Inc.
